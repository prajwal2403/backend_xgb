About The Test

The Big 5 Ocean Test

The Big Five (OCEAN) personality test measures five broad personality traits that are believed to account for differences in decision making and personality:

Openness

How open a person is to new experiences, ranging from curious and inventive to cautious and consistent

Conscientiousness

How organized, disciplined, and reliable a person is, ranging from efficient and organized to extravagant and careless

Extroversion

How outgoing and energetic a person is, ranging from outgoing and energetic to solitary and reserved

Agreeableness

Individuals high in agreeableness tend to be compassionate, cooperative, and empathetic toward others.

Neuroticism

People high in neuroticism are often more prone to anxiety, emotional instability, and negative emotions.
